Dogs

A dog (scientific name: Canis lupus familiaris) is a domesticated carnivorous mammal. Often called "man's best friend," it is characterized by four legs, a tail, and keen senses. Dogs were the first animals domesticated by humans, evolving from an extinct wolf ancestor thousands of years ago.

Dogs, often called "man's best friend," are the most popular domesticated pets globally. Descended from an extinct species of wolf, they were the first animals tamed by humans over 20,000 years ago. Known for their incredible loyalty, they come in over 400 distinct breeds and are celebrated for their intelligence and remarkable senses.

Food Habitats

Establish healthy routines by feeding 2-3 controlled meals daily at set times rather than free-feeding, which prevents fussy eating. Balance their diet using high-quality commercial kibble rich in proteins, or vet-approved fresh diets, while ensuring they always have access to fresh drinking water.
